China Culture

A country's cultural values shape its national identity and collective mindset. These values and public sentiments are communicated to government leaders via the media and other channels, influencing foreign policy decisions both directly and indirectly. In Chinese culture, traditional values such as harmony, benevolence, righteousness, courtesy, wisdom, honesty, loyalty, and filial piety play a significant role in shaping the attitudes and perspectives of the Chinese people.
Benevolence, a central principle of Confucianism, stems from the emphasis on familial bonds and blood relationships, which are deeply valued in Chinese culture. A popular saying, "A peaceful family leads to prosperity (jiahe wanshi xing, 家和万事兴)," reflects this belief. While rooted in family connections, this sense of benevolence also extends to friendships and social interactions, fostering a broader set of values such as justice, courtesy, wisdom, honesty, loyalty, self-discipline, and commitment.
