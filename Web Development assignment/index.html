Introduction of China

China holds the distinction of having the longest history of any nation. More than 2,000 years ago, it had already established a powerful state with a centralized government governing the largest territory the world had ever seen. Remarkably, even at that early period, China had a historical tradition dating back another 2,000 years. Although periods of division and regional governance have occasionally fragmented the country, the prevailing belief in a unified Chinese identity and culture has consistently driven reunification, making these interruptions seem like temporary anomalies in the narrative of one of the oldest political entities in existence.China also stands as the most populous and culturally diverse nation. Its nearly 1.4 billion inhabitants include numerous minority groups, some of whom do not identify as ethnically Chinese. The country spans a vast range of landscapes, juxtaposing ultra-modern urban centers with rural areas that retain premodern lifestyles.
